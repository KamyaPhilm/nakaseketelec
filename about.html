History

The establishment of the Telecentre started way back in 1997 as a small Resource Center which later with the support from UNESCO, ITU and IDRC expanded into a bigger Telecenter.
The Telecentre was opened on 5th March 1999 by the Minister of Gender, Labour and Youth Social Development represented by the Minister of state in Charge of Childrean and Youth, Hon. Kakembo Mary Nsangi, Members of the Community, District Administrators, Local Leaders, Project Partners and Members of the Donor Community attended the occasion.

It was well covered in the print and electronic media which provided commendable publicity to the Telecentre. The Nakaseke Telecentre is found in Nakaseke Town Council and owned by Nakaseke Sub-country, Nakaseke Town council and Kasangombe Sub-county.
The project was built on a strong foundation of management with 16 members selected from the local councils of Nakaseke and Kasangombe Sub-counties to represent the interest of the community members (Farmers, Women, Youth, Educationist, the Civil Society and the Business Community).

Background

Nakaseke Multipurpose Community Telecentre is a part of a series of pilot initiatives to introduce, test, disseminate and assess the impact and viability of Multipurpose Community Telecentres (MCTs) in Africa being planned within the framework of the Africa Information Society Initiative (AISI) and in line with the Buenos Aires Action Plan (BAAP) PROGRAMME No. 9 on Integrated Rural Development.
The International Telecommunications Union (ITU), UNESCO and IDRC agreed to join efforts in partnering with National and Local Organizations in this process and to plan and support pilot Telecentres.

The partners in Nakaseke MCT are both International and National. They include United Nations Educational Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO using DANIDA funds in trust), International Development Research Centre (IDRC) and International Telecommunications Union (ITU) on the one Hand and Uganda National Commission for UNESCO, Public Libraries Board now the National Library of Uganda, British Council in Uganda , the two Sub-counties Nakaseke and Kasangombe and Local Governments.
The project was executed by Uganda National Commission for UNESCO for a period of three years since December, 1997.

The objectives is to Develop and Test a MCT model to facilitate a process of development which is affordable and accessible for Rural Communities and provides Basic Telephone Communication and Computing facility; access to and production of information resources relevant to specific local users; and training in ICTs.
The pilot project has developed modalities to support sustainability and has assessed the social economic and cultural impact with regard to the community and contribution towards national and international understanding concerning the importance of information and telecommunication for development.

Project support

Renovation, equipment & furniture installation:- This included boosting the Nakaseke Radio Signal, recruitment of the Resource center Support personnel, purchase and delivery of computers, a generator and renovation of the telecenter offices.

Capacity building:- This includes entrepreneurship skills and mobile money usage trainings, development of training manuals and provision of business handbooks for the business community.

Marketing platform:- This computer software is to help in bridging information gaps by allowing farmers to access current market prices in a common marketing platform in order to make informed decisions about their selling prices. It also enables farmers to alert potential large scale buyers of the availability of their produce, where they are and in what quantities.

Radio programs:- These programs are in the areas of agriculture, entrepreneurship, gender equality, and family issues.
